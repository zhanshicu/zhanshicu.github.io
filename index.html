About Me

Education

I am a student from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen. I am currently studying Data Science and Big Data Technology. I participated the University London College (UCL) Summer School in 2023.
I am the recipient of the 21st and the 22nd Undergraduate Research Award (URA) of CUHK-Shenzhen, and the Dean's List of 2022-2023 academic year.

Research

My research experience includes on-street parking solutions (supervisor: Prof. Ruizhi Liao), clustering algorithms (supervisor: Prof. Wenyi Li), speaker recognition (supervisor: Prof. Haizhou Li), and big data marketing (supervisor: Prof. Li Yang).
I am interested in exploring how machine learning or deep learning methods can provide insights to marketing strategy.

My Projects

Ongoing Projects:

Video Advertising Difference between Luxurious and Mainstream Products (Research Assistant; Supervisor: Prof. Li Yang)

  • Investigated the impact of product commercials on its luxurious/mainstream brand positioning;
  • Employed a dual-encoder framework to quantify the brand-commercial alignment, and then developed a binary classification model to validate findings.

Completed Projects:

Connection between Brand Identities and Advertisement Video Utterances (Course Project: Big Data Marketing)

  • Led a team and directed methodology development and presentations;
  • Extracted voiceover representations for various brands using a pre-trained model, while investigating their correlations with brand positioning and identity.

Deep Learning for Speaker Recognition (Research Assistant; Supervisor: Prof. Haizhou Li)

  • Developed algorithms for a more robust speaker recognition model: a data cleansing method using an audio-visual model, and an augmentation method using voice conversion;
  • Utlized an audio-visual system with perfect Equal-Error-Rate (EER) to filter out noisy samples.

Clustering with Incomplete Observation (Research Assistant; Supervisor: Prof. Wenye Li)

  • Developed algorithms for multi-view clustering and spectral clustering on incomplete data;
  • Constructed a similarity graph using a high-quality affinity measure that models the local neighborhood relations among data samples.

Models for On-Street Parking Solutions (Research Assistant; Supervisor: Prof. Ruizhi Liao)

  • Proposed a general evaluation model for different parking solutions, including fixed sensing and mobile sensing;
  • Developed a R-N algorithm to enhance the performance of on-street parking solutions and extend the evaluation model using queueing theory.
